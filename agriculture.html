IoT in agriculture, often called Smart Farming or Agri-Tech, involves using sensors, drones, robots, and data analytics to optimize farming practices. By deploying smart sensors in fields, farmers can collect real-time data on soil moisture, nutrient levels, weather conditions, and crop health. This data empowers them to make informed decisions, automate tasks like irrigation, and apply resources exactly when and where they are needed, minimizing waste and maximizing efficiency.

Key Applications

Precision Farming

Use GPS-guided tractors and drones to apply fertilizers, pesticides, and water with surgical precision, reducing costs and environmental impact.

Smart Irrigation

Soil moisture sensors trigger irrigation systems automatically, ensuring crops get the exact amount of water needed and conserving up to 50% of water.

Crop Monitoring

Drones equipped with multispectral cameras monitor crop health, detect diseases early, and help create accurate yield predictions.

Livestock Tracking

Wearable sensors on cattle monitor their health, location, and activity levels, enabling early detection of illness and optimizing breeding patterns.
